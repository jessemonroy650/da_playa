In house, we monitor thirty (30) to one-hundred (100) video or podcasts from youtube, rumble, soundcloud or podbean daily. The youtube website is bad, horrible, hideous; rumble, soundcloud & podbean are not much better. Instead of improving their search functions, they try to force feed the users garbage-content.

Instructions

  1. ENCODER convert a url into a CSV for processing
  2. LOADER load the CSV into the player
  3. VIDEO PLAYER play

1. ENCODER

With youtube.com, use the URL.
With rumble.com & soundcloud.com, use the EMBED button to find the appropriate URL.

2. LOADER

  1. The list of encoded URLS is put into the 'CVS Input'
  2. Press the button
  3. The list will then be update for internal use and displayed in the FINAL section.
  4. Switch window, you are ready to play.

3. VIDEO PLAYER

  1. Hit button
  2. Hit the browser Reload page button.
  3. Hit play button on video, if it does not automatically start.

4. EXTENSION

  1. rumble_buttons - in embedded mode, only the spacebar works as the play/pause on the keyboard.
  2. notepad - keeps notes on the page until the button is hit.
  3. time_clock - visible running clock, required for time_alarm.
  4. time_alarm - If you are waiting for a particular show or event, a one-time (audio or alert()) alarm is available.
  5. sound_alarm - One-time sound (audio) for the alarm is available; requires time_clock, time_alarm.
  6. working - simulate_click - simulate mouse click at x/y
  7. coming - jump - The button jumps directly to the next video.
  8. coming - extended - information about video, including thumbnails
  9. coming - titleList - a text list of the title of upcoming media clips
  10. coming - useEmbedList - use the embedded player's internal list function when possible.
  11. coming - journal - when you viewed or listened
  12. coming - bookmark - with notes
  13. coming - thirdParty - annotations
  14. coming - preferredSource - based on annotations, to distiguish the exact same content on different platforms
  15. coming - defaultAudioLevel - some browsers require audio to be off to start autoplay

5. SIDE EFFECTS

  1. If you logout of youtube, the commercials will mostly go away since they don't know who you are.
    This also means you CANNOT give a video a thumbs-up/down or leave comments
  2. How autoplay works changes depending on which webbrowser and which version you are using. All the major webbrowsers (Chrome, Firefox, Explorer, and Safari) have all gone back and forth between allowing and NOT allowing.
